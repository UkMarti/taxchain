# Netherlands — 30% Ruling — v1.0

## Quick reference

| Item | Value |
|---|---|
| Benefit | **30% of salary** (including the allowance) paid tax-free as cost reimbursement |
| Effective top rate reduction | From 49.5% to ~34.65% on gross salary |
| Duration | **5 years** (from 2024; previously 5 years, was 8 before 2019) |
| Salary threshold (2025) | Minimum €46,660 gross taxable salary (€41,954 for under-30 with master's) |
| Application | Employer applies to Belastingdienst within 4 months of start of employment |
| Legislation | Wet IB 2001 Art. 10e–10ef; Uitvoeringsbesluit LB 2001 |

---

## How it works

The 30% ruling allows a Dutch employer to pay an employee an **income tax-free
allowance** of up to 30% of the **total remuneration** (including the allowance itself)
as reimbursement of extraterritorial costs (housing, school fees, travel to home country).

In practice: of the gross salary, 30% is treated as a tax-free reimbursement and
70% is the taxable wage.

```
Example: €100,000 gross salary
  30% tax-free allowance = €30,000
  Taxable wage = €70,000
  Tax at top rate 49.5% = €34,650
  Effective rate on €100,000 = 34.65% (vs 49.5% without ruling)
```

---

## Eligibility conditions

1. **Recruited from abroad**: the employee must be recruited or transferred from
   outside the Netherlands
2. **Lived more than 150 km from Dutch border** for at least 16 of the 24 months
   before starting work in the Netherlands
3. **Specific expertise**: salary must exceed the statutory threshold (€46,660 in 2025)
4. **Dutch employer** sponsoring the application

---

## Partial non-resident taxation (keuzerecht)

30% ruling holders can elect to be treated as a **partial non-resident** of the
Netherlands for Box 2 and Box 3 purposes:
- Box 2 (substantial interests in Dutch companies): taxed as resident
- Box 3 (savings and investment wealth tax): only Dutch real estate taxed — foreign
  assets excluded from the Dutch wealth tax base

This is a significant benefit for high-net-worth individuals with foreign investment
portfolios.

The partial non-resident election must be made on the annual tax return.

---

## 2024 changes

From **1 January 2024**, the rules changed:
- Duration reduced to **5 years** (not extended beyond that)
- The 30% rate applies in full for years 1–5

Previously (the old transitional regime): the rate stepped down from 30% → 20% → 10%
in years 4 and 5. The full 30% for all 5 years is an improvement for new applicants.

---

## After the ruling expires

When the 5-year ruling period ends, the employee becomes a fully taxable Dutch resident:
- Taxed at normal progressive rates on worldwide income
- All Box 3 wealth included in the Dutch tax base
- Plan ahead for year 6 — the income tax hit can be significant
