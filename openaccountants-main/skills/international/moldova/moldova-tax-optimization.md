> **Moldova's headline is the IT PARK 7% regime:** a single **7% tax on turnover** (excl. VAT) replaces corporate tax, income tax, social contributions and several others — for IT businesses. Savings can reach 20–50% of the total tax burden.

## Section 2 -- IT Park (7% of turnover)

| Feature | Detail |
|---|---|
| Tax | **7% of turnover** (excl. VAT) — replaces CIT, employee income tax, social/health contributions, and more |
| Legal form | **SRL (LLC)** or **ÎI (sole proprietor)** |
| Eligibility | **≥ 70% of revenue** from eligible IT activities (software development, IT services, etc.) |
| Best for | Software/IT firms with significant payroll (the 7% covers payroll taxes too) |

## Section 3 -- 4% Small-Business Turnover Regime

- SMEs **not registered for VAT** that meet the criteria may opt for a **4% tax on aggregate income (turnover)** — simple, no profit computation. Best for low-expense small businesses below the VAT threshold.

---

## Section 4 -- Standard System & Extraction (SRL / ÎI)

| Item | Rate |
|---|---|
| Corporate / personal income tax | **12%** |
| Dividends to founders | **6%** |
| Sole proprietor (ÎI) | 12% on income; simplest for solo freelancers |
| SRL (LLC) | 12% CIT; recommended for employees/international corporate clients |

Owner can withdraw profit annually as **6% dividends** instead of salary — model salary-vs-dividend with the contribution effect.

## Section 5 -- Red Lines (do not cross)

- **IT Park 70% test:** at least 70% of revenue must be genuine eligible IT activity — non-IT income placed in an IT Park entity is a misuse. **AUDIT FLASH POINT**.
- **Substance & genuine self-employment:** single-client ÎI under employer-like control → reclassification.
- Don't route non-eligible income through the 4%/7% regimes.

---

## PROHIBITIONS

- NEVER place non-IT revenue in the IT Park 7% regime to dodge tax (70% eligibility test).
- NEVER present the 4% regime for a VAT-registered business — it is for non-VAT SMEs.
- NEVER present single-client work as safe self-employment.
- NEVER contradict the rates in `moldova-income-tax.md` / `moldova-social-contributions.md`.
