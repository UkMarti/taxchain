# Spain — Beckham Law (RETD) — v1.0

## Quick reference

| Item | Value |
|---|---|
| Official name | Régimen Especial para Trabajadores Desplazados (RETD) |
| Flat rate | **24%** on Spanish-source income up to €600,000; 47% above |
| Foreign employment income | Exempt (if work performed outside Spain) |
| Foreign investment income | Taxable at savings rates (19%–28%) |
| Duration | 6 tax years (year of becoming resident + 5 following) |
| Eligibility gap | Not tax resident in Spain in the 5 years prior |
| Legislation | LIRPF Art. 93; RIRPF Arts. 113–120 |
| Application | Form 149 — must apply within 6 months of Spanish social security registration |

---

## Who qualifies

The RETD (Beckham Law) is available to individuals who:
1. Become Spanish tax residents due to **moving to Spain for work**
2. Have **not been Spanish tax resident in the 5 years** prior to arrival
3. Are employed by a Spanish company or posted to Spain by a foreign employer, OR
   (since 2023 reform) are:
   - Remote workers for foreign companies ("digital nomads")
   - Entrepreneurs starting a business in Spain
   - Highly qualified professionals working for "emerging companies"
   - Individuals managing qualified investment in Spain

---

## Tax rates under RETD

| Income type | RETD treatment |
|---|---|
| Spanish employment income (≤ €600,000) | **24% flat rate** |
| Spanish employment income (> €600,000) | 47% on excess |
| Foreign employment income (work outside Spain) | **Exempt** |
| Spanish dividends, interest, capital gains | Savings income rates: 19% (up to €6k), 21% (€6k–€50k), 23% (€50k–€200k), 27% (€200k–€300k), 28% (above €300k) |
| Foreign dividends, interest, capital gains | **Taxable** at savings rates (not exempt) |
| Foreign rental income | Taxable |

**Key limitation**: Only Spanish-employment and Spanish-business income is taxed at the
flat 24%. Foreign passive income (dividends, interest, capital gains) is taxed at
standard savings rates — the same as ordinary residents.

---

## Application process

1. Register with Spanish Social Security or obtain NIE
2. File **Form 149** (opting into the regime) within **6 months** of the date of
   registration with Social Security (or the start date of the activity in Spain)
3. Employer withholds at the 24% flat rate
4. File **Form 151** (simplified non-resident IRPF return) by 30 June of the
   following year instead of the standard Form 100

Missing the 6-month window = permanent exclusion for that stay in Spain.

---

## Renouncing the regime

The taxpayer can voluntarily renounce the RETD from 1 January of any year during the
6-year period. After renunciation, they return to ordinary resident taxation.

---

## Digital nomad visa (2023 onwards)

Spain introduced a **digital nomad visa** in 2023. Holders who become Spanish tax
residents are explicitly eligible for the Beckham regime. Key conditions:
- Work for non-Spanish companies (at least mainly)
- Can work for Spanish clients up to 20% of total income
