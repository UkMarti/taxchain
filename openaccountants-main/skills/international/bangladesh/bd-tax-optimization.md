## Section 2 — The levers (Tier 1)
1. **Investment tax rebate.** Individuals get a rebate on eligible investments (DPS/savings certificates, life insurance, approved funds) — typically the lower of a % of eligible investment, a % of taxable income, or a cap. This directly reduces tax — **verify the current rates/caps**.
2. **File the e-Return and keep your TIN active.** Non-filers/those without proof of return submission face **higher withholding (TDS)** and are barred from many services. Filing is often the cheapest saving.
3. **Manage minimum tax.** Even with low income, a TIN holder crossing certain thresholds pays a **minimum tax** (৳3,000–৳5,000 by location) — plan around it.
4. **ICT/ITES export cash incentive.** Route export earnings through banking channels to capture any government cash incentive on ICT/ITES remittances (verify current %).
5. **Document expenses** for domestic business income (taxed on net profit under the slabs).

## Section 3 — Worked persona
IT freelancer, ৳3,000,000 export income: post-June-2024 this is taxable (verify any reduced ICT rate) — file the e-Return, claim the **investment rebate** on DPS/insurance, capture the **export cash incentive** on the remittance, and stay a filer to avoid higher TDS elsewhere.

## Section 4 — Risks & red flags
- The IT/ITES exemption **expired 30 June 2024** — don't plan on it; verify any successor relief.
- Receiving export pay outside banking channels forfeits the incentive and risks penalties.

## Section 10 — Prohibitions
- NEVER assume the lapsed IT exemption still applies.
- NEVER advise informal/off-banking receipt of export income.
- NEVER state rebate/incentive rates without verifying the current Finance Act / Bangladesh Bank circular.
