## Section 2 — Key points (Tier 1)
- Bangladesh has **no broad mandatory social-security contribution for the self-employed**. A solo freelancer is generally **not** required to pay social contributions — the main obligation is income tax (and, for exporters, see bd-it-freelancer-tax). State this clearly.
- The **Universal Pension Scheme** (launched 2023) is **voluntary**: a self-employed person can join the **Surokkha** scheme and contribute monthly toward a pension; the **Samata** scheme adds a government co-contribution for low-income earners. Verify current contribution tiers and the government match.
- **Employees** may have a **provident fund** and **gratuity** through their employer; there is no universal national social-insurance payroll tax like in many countries.

## Section 3 — Worked example
A solo freelancer: **no** mandatory social contribution; income tax only. Optionally joins **Surokkha** under the Universal Pension Scheme for a voluntary pension (and the contribution may qualify toward the investment tax rebate — verify).

## Section 10 — Prohibitions
- NEVER tell a solo freelancer they owe mandatory social security (generally they do not) — flag the voluntary pension option.
- NEVER state pension contribution tiers / government match without verifying current NPA rules.
