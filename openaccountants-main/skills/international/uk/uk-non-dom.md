## Section 2 — The New FIG Regime (From April 2025)

The UK abolished the remittance basis / domicile-based non-dom regime from **6 April 2025**.

Replacement: the **4-year Foreign Income and Gains (FIG) exemption**.

**Who qualifies:**
- Any individual who becomes a UK tax resident on or after 6 April 2025
- AND was **not UK tax resident in any of the preceding 10 tax years**

**What is exempt:**
- All **foreign income** (dividends, interest, rent, employment income from foreign employer) arising while the individual is in their first 4 years of UK tax residence
- All **foreign capital gains** arising while in the first 4 years

**What is NOT exempt:**
- UK-source income and gains — taxed normally regardless of FIG status
- Foreign income/gains arising AFTER the 4-year window

**The FIG window is absolute**: no extension, no exceptions. After 4 tax years of UK residence, the individual is taxed on worldwide basis from year 5 onwards.

**No election needed**: FIG applies automatically. The individual may elect OUT (e.g. if they want to use foreign losses against UK income) but there is no requirement to file a special claim.

## Section 3 — Transitional Provisions: Existing Non-Doms

For individuals who were **already using the remittance basis** before 6 April 2025:

**Option 1 — Temporary Repatriation Facility (TRF):**
A 3-year window (2025/26, 2026/27, 2027/28) to bring previously unremitted foreign income and gains to the UK at a **reduced flat rate**:
- 12% for amounts remitted in 2025/26 and 2026/27
- 15% for amounts remitted in 2027/28

After 2027/28, unremitted pre-2025 income remitted to the UK is taxed at full normal rates.

**Option 2 — Rebasing of foreign capital gains:**
Foreign assets (other than non-UK real estate) can be **rebased to their 5 April 2019 market value** for CGT purposes. Only applies to individuals who claimed the remittance basis in 2016/17 or earlier.

## Section 4 — Before April 2025: Remittance Basis (History)

The old regime (now abolished for new arrivals) worked as follows:
- UK residents who were **not domiciled in the UK** could elect for the **remittance basis**
- Foreign income and gains were taxed only when **remitted to the UK**
- Annual remittance basis charge: £30,000 (after 7 years UK residence) / £60,000 (after 12 years)
- After 15 years UK residence: deemed domiciled — remittance basis no longer available

This system is now history for new residents. The FIG regime replaced it.

---

## Section 5 — Overseas Workday Relief (OWR)

**Overseas Workday Relief (OWR)** continues under the new regime.

OWR allows UK-resident employees to exclude from UK tax the **proportion of employment income** attributable to non-UK workdays (days physically working outside the UK).

Under the new FIG regime, OWR is available for the **first 4 years** of UK residence (aligned with FIG), without the remittance condition that applied under the old regime.

**Formula:**
```
UK-taxable employment income = Total employment income × (UK workdays / Total workdays)
```

Requires keeping a contemporaneous workday log.
